損 Sǔn / Decrease

above:Gèn Keeping Still, Mountain
below:Duì The Joyous, Lake

The second [nine], undivided, shows that it will be advantageous for its subject to maintain a firm correctness, and that action on his part will be evil. He can give increase (to his correlate) without taking from himself.

Line 2 is strong, and in the central place. But it is in the place of a weak line, and its subject should maintain his position without moving to help its correlate in 5. Maintaining his own firm correctness is the best way to help him.
