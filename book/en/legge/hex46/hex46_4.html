升 Shēng / Pushing Upward

above:Kūn The Receptive, Earth
below:Xùn The Gentle, Wind

The fourth [six], divided, shows its subject employed by the king to present his offerings on Mount Chi. There will be good fortune; there will be no mistake.

Line 4 occupies the place of a great minister, in immediate contiguity to his ruler, who confides in him, and raises him to the highest distinction as a feudal prince. The mention of Mount Chi, at the foot of which was the capital of the lords of Zhōu, seems to take the paragraph out of the sphere of symbolism and into that of history. 'The king' in it is the last sovereign of Shang; the feudal prince in it is Wén.
