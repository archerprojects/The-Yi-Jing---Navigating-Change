履 Lǚ / Treading [Conduct]

above: Qián The Creative, Heaven
below: Duì The Joyous, Lake

The second [nine], undivided, shows its subject treading the path that is level and easy; a quiet and solitary man, to whom, if he be firm and correct, there will be good fortune.

Line 2 occupies the middle place in the trigram, which is supposed to symbolize a path cut straight and level along the hillside, or over difficult ground. Line 5 is not a proper correlate, and hence the idea of the subject of 2 being 'a quiet and solitary man'.
