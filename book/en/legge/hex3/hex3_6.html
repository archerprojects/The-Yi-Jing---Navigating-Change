屯 Zhūn / Difficulty at the Beginning

above: Kǎn The Abysmal, Water
below: Zhèn The Arousing, Thunder

The topmost [six], divided, shows (its subject) with the horses of his chariot obliged to retreat, and weeping tears of blood in streams.

The sixth line is weak; the third responding to it is also weak; it is at the extremity of peril; the game is up. What can remain for its subject in such a case but error and abject weeping?
