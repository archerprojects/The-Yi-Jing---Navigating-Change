坤 Kūn / The Receptive

above: Kūn The Receptive, Earth
below: Kūn The Receptive, Earth

The fifth [six], divided, (shows) the yellow lower garment. There will be great good fortune.

There is an interpretation of the symbolism of paragraph 5 in a narrative of the Cho Kwan, under the 12th year of the Duke Zhāo, B.C. 530. 'Yellow' is one of the five 'correct' colors, and the color of the Earth. 'The lower garment' is a symbol of humility. The fifth line is the seat of honor. If its occupant possess the qualities indicated, he will be greatly fortunate.
