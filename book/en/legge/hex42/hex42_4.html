益 Yì / Increase

above:Xùn The Gentle, Wind
below:Zhèn The Arousing, Thunder

The fourth [six], divided, shows its subject pursuing the due course. His advice to the prince is followed. He can with advantage be relied on in such a movement as that of removing the capital.

Line 4 is the place for a minister, near to that of the ruler. Its subject is weak, but his place is appropriate, and as he follows the due course, his ruler will listen to him, and he will be a support in the most critical movements. Changing the capital from place to place was frequent in the feudal times of China. That of Shang, which preceded Zhōu, was changed five times.
