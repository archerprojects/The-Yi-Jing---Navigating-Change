旅 Lǚ / The Wanderer

above: Zhèn The Arousing, Thunder
below: Lí The Clinging, Flame

The third [nine], undivided, shows the stranger, burning his lodging-house, and having lost his servants. However firm and correct he (try to) be, he will be in peril.

Line 3 is strong, and in an even place. But it occupies the topmost place in the lower trigram; and its strength may be expected to appear as violence. So it does in the symbolism, and extraordinary violence as well. It seems unreasonable to suppose, as in the conclusion, that one so described could be in any way correct. The Kāngxī editors remark that the subjects of 2 and 3 are represented as having 'lodging-houses', and not any of those of the other lines, because these are the only two lines in the places proper to them!
