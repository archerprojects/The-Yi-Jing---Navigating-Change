震 Zhèn / The Arousing (Shock, Thunder)

above:Zhèn The Arousing, Thunder
below:Zhèn The Arousing, Thunder

The first [nine], undivided, shows its subject, when the movement approaches, looking out and around with apprehension, and afterwards smiling and talking cheerfully. There will be good fortune.

What is said on line 1 is little more than a repetition of the principal part of the Tuàn. The line is undivided, and gives the auspice of good fortune.
