鼎 50

彖传

鼎，象也。以木巽火，亨饪也。圣人亨以享上帝，而大亨以养圣贤。巽而耳目聪明，柔进而上行，得中而应乎刚，是以元亨。

In Dǐng we have (symbolically) the figure of a caldron. (We see) the (symbol of) wood entering into that of fire, which suggests the idea of cooking. The sages cooked their offerings in order to present them to God, and made great feasts to nourish their wise and able (ministers). We have the symbol of) flexible obedience, and that (which denotes) ears quick of hearing and eyes clear-sighted. (We have also) the weak (line) advanced and acting above, in the central place, and responded to by the strong (line below). All these things give the idea of 'great progress and success.'

象传（大象）

木上有火，鼎；君子以正位凝命。

(The trigram representing) wood and above it that for fire form Dǐng. The superior man, in accordance with this, keeps his every position correct, and maintains secure the appointment (of Heaven).

序卦

革物者莫若鼎，故受之以《鼎》。

For changing the substance of things there is nothing equal to the caldron; and hence Gé is followed by Dǐng.

杂卦

《鼎》，取新也。

Dǐng takes what's new.
