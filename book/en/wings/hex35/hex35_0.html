晉 35

彖传

晋，进也。明出地上，顺而丽乎大明，柔进而上行。是以康侯用锡马蕃庶，昼日三接也。

Jìn denotes advancing. (In Jìn we have) the bright (sun) appearing above the earth; (the symbol of) docile submission cleaving to that of the Great brightness; and the weak line advanced and moving above: - all these things give us the idea of 'a prince who secures the tranquillity (of the people), presented on that account with numerous horses (by the king), and three times in a day received at interviews.'

象传（大象）

明出地上，晋；君子以自昭明德。

(The trigram representing) the earth and that for the bright (sun) coming forth above it form Jìn. The superior man, according to this, gives himself to make more brilliant his bright virtue.

序卦

物不可以终壮，故受之以《晋》。

Things cannot remain forever (simply) in the state of vigour; and hence Dà Zhuàng is succeeded by Jìn.

杂卦

《晋》，昼也。
