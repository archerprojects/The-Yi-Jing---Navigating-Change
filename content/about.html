Software Lineage

Derived from iching-0.2 by Jean-Pierre Charalambos (2002).

Coin-casting engine from pyChing by Stephen M. Gava (1999–2006).

Port, redesign, and additional engineering: archerprojects, 2026.

Translations

Richard Wilhelm — German rendering (1923/24), translated into English by Cary F. Baynes (1950).

James Legge — The Yî King, Sacred Books of the East, Vol. XVI (Oxford, 1882; rev. 1899). Public domain.

Bradford Hatcher — The Book of Changes: Yijing, Word by Word, Vols. 1–2 (Hermetica.info, Nucla CO, 2009). Character-by-character and literal translation with the Rogue River commentary, distributed freely by the author.

Classical Chinese text — the 周易 Zhōuyì (judgment and line texts) together with the Ten Wings, 十翼 Shí Yì. Public-domain source; the delivered text follows Chinese Wikisource.

Fonts

AR PL UKai CN — © Arphic Technology Co., Ltd, under the Arphic Public License; part of the freedesktop.org CJK Unifonts. Used here as a Latin-stripped subset so that Han glyphs render in the calligraphic kǎi hand while Western text falls to the serif body face.

青柳隷書しも (Aoyagi Reisho Shimo) — calligraphy (揮毫) by Aoyagi Kōzan (青柳衡山), digitized by SIMO, who holds the copyright. A free font, supplied here as an extended subset for traditional display glyphs; the author's original documentation travels with it in fonts/simo_docs/ as the licence requires.
