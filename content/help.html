User Guide 易經

Getting Started — For first time users

Select + New profile to establish an alias and create a resident profile that will store journal entries of your casting history. Guests can enter without creating a profile, but those readings are not saved to a journal. To start a reading, select Profile or Guest on the opening screen, pick a saved Profile or enter as Guest. Filling in your profile (Journal → Profile) — date and time of birth, birth location, current location — gives each reading a richer, more personal frame: those details are captured and shown beside the reading in your journal. NO INFORMATION IS SHARED OR SENT OUTSIDE THE APP.

The Questions

Before the oracle opens you answer four things: your Question, a Purpose (pick any that fit), your Mood (pick one), and the day's Weather/conditions (pick any). The Proceed to the Oracle button stays locked until a profile or Guest is selected and all four are answered — the casting controls are held until you've settled the question you're bringing. Completing them opens a fresh journal entry that records the moment.

Casting Methods

  • Three coins — the quick, familiar method; each throw of three coins gives one line, building the hexagram from the bottom up.
  • Yarrow stalks — the older, slower ritual division, animated stalk by stalk; its line probabilities differ from the coins, as tradition holds.
  • Manual Cast Entry — record a reading you cast by hand, away from the app.
  • Open Reading — reload a saved .ich reading.

Switch methods freely before the first line; switching is blocked mid-cast (you'll be asked to finish first); once a reading is complete, choosing a method begins a new one.

Reading the Result

The hexagram assembles line by line; any changing lines produce the relating hexagram. Read it across five sources — Wilhelm, Legge, Hatcher, 中文, and the Ten Wings / 十翼.

Study

The Study menu opens the texts to browse on their own.

The Hexagram Wheel sets all 64 hexagrams in a ring, with three ways to open one:

  • Drag — click the wheel and drag to spin it; release and it slows, settles on a hexagram, and opens it.
  • Spin button — click ↻ Spin the Wheel to spin it at random; it slows, settles on a hexagram, and opens it.
  • Click — click any hexagram on the wheel to open it directly.

Hovering over a hexagram shows its number, character, and name in the side panel.

Alongside the Wheel, Study holds the Ten Wings treatises, each on its own page — 繫辭上 / 繫辭下 (the Great Treatise, I and II), 說卦 (Discussion of the Trigrams), 序卦 (the Sequence of the Hexagrams), and 雜卦 (the Hexagrams in Miscellaneous order) — and Trigram Lookup, where you choose an upper and a lower trigram and it identifies and opens the hexagram they form.

The Journal

For profile users every completed reading is saved automatically — your question, purpose, mood and weather; the auto-captured moment (moon phase, date, time, location); and your profile context. In View Journal you can filter and search your entries, select one to see its full detail with the hexagram text in zoomable Wilhelm / Legge / Hatcher panels, and right-click any entry to Read it in the reader, Edit it, or Delete it. Add Notes attaches a reflection and tags to your most recent reading. Profile lets you edit your details — and from a Guest session, create a profile; the reading you just cast is carried into it as its first entry. Finished readings export with Export to PDF.

Keyboard Shortcuts

Ctrl+N — new reading · Ctrl+S — export to PDF · Ctrl+Q — quit.

Privacy

Everything — profiles, journals, and readings — is stored only on your own machine. No information is shared or sent outside the app.
