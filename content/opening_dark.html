The YiJing — Navigating Change

The YiJing (I Ching) 易經 “The Book of Changes” is an ancient Chinese text used as a guide for reflection and understanding the patterns of change in life. Its core structure consists of 64 hexagrams, each made of six lines that are either broken (陰 yin) or unbroken (陽 yang), symbolizing different states and transitions. To consult it, coins or yarrow stalks are cast to generate a hexagram, which is then interpreted to offer philosophical insight. Rather than foretell a rigid future, it encourages thoughtful awareness of shifting circumstances and one’s place within them.
